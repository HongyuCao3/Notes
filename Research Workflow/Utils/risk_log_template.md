# Risk Log Template

---

## Risk ID
`R<n>_<short_description>`

---

## Risk Category
Conceptual / Claim-related / Baseline-related / Experimental / Reproducibility / Evaluation / Reviewer expectation / Scope

---

## Risk Description
One concrete, specific statement.
> Example: "A recent method may subsume our approach under a different formulation."

---

## Affected Components
- Claim IDs:
- Experiment Family IDs:
- Paper sections:
- Figures/tables:

---

## Trigger / Signal
How this risk was identified: literature discovery, baseline result, experiment outcome, reviewer comment, internal discussion.

---

## Severity
- **Low**: minor wording or formatting change needed
- **Medium**: requires claim scoping or additional experiments
- **High**: threatens core contribution or novelty

---

## Likelihood
Unlikely / Possible / Likely

---

## Risk Exposure
Low / Medium / High (severity × likelihood)

---

## Mitigation Strategy
Concrete actions: add baseline family, restrict claim scope, reframe contribution, add ablation or stress test.

---

## Mitigation Status
Planned / In Progress / Implemented / Not Applicable

---

## Residual Risk
None / Acceptable / High but justified

---

## Writing Implications
Claim wording adjustments, limitation section emphasis, figure selection, contribution tone.

---

## Decision Log
> Example: "Accepted weaker IID performance and emphasized robustness instead."

---

## Review Cycle Notes
*(Optional)* How this risk evolved across rounds; reviewer feedback; changes between versions.

---

## Status
Open / Monitoring / Mitigated / Accepted / Closed

---

## Final Check (Before Submission)
- Risk is mitigated or explicitly accepted
- Implications reflected in claims and writing
- No contradictions between risk analysis and paper
