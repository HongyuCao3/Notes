# **Ad hoc vs. Post hoc (in academic writing & peer review)**

## **1. Ad hoc**

**Literal**: “for this purpose”

### **Reviewer meaning**

> ⚠️ *Methodologically unprincipled or improvised*

### **Signal you’re sending (often unintentionally)**

* Tailored to fit current data/problem
* Lacks theoretical grounding or prior justification
* Not generalizable / not pre-specified

### **Common reviewer phrases**

* *ad hoc assumption / parameter / adjustment*
* *ad hoc modeling choice*

### **What they really question**

* Why this design?
* Would it work beyond this dataset?
* Is this just “curve fitting with a story”?

### **How to neutralize**

* Anchor in **theory or literature**
* Show **robustness across settings**
* Clarify **design rationale (not arbitrary)**

---

## **2. Post hoc**

**Literal**: “after this”

### **Reviewer meaning**

> ⚠️ *Result-driven reasoning (retrospective justification)*

---

### **Two critical uses**

#### (1) **Post hoc analysis**

* Conducted after seeing the data

👉 Risk:

* multiple testing / p-hacking
* lack of pre-registration

---

#### (2) **Post hoc explanation**

* Hypothesis formed *after* observing results

👉 Risk:

* HARKing (Hypothesizing After Results are Known)
* narrative built to fit outcomes

---

### **Common reviewer phrases**

* *post hoc analysis*
* *post hoc explanation*
* *appears to be post hoc*

### **What they really question**

* Was this planned or improvised?
* Are you inferring causality from patterns?
* Would this hold under pre-specified testing?

---

### **How to neutralize**

* Distinguish clearly:

  * **confirmatory vs. exploratory**
* Pre-specify hypotheses when possible
* If post hoc:

  * **label it explicitly + avoid strong claims**

---

# **Core Contrast (fast recall)**

|               | **Ad hoc**                         | **Post hoc**                              |
| ------------- | ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Focus         | Method/design                      | Reasoning/interpretation                  |
| Problem       | Arbitrary construction             | Retrospective justification               |
| Reviewer fear | “You made this up to make it work” | “You explained this after seeing results” |
| Failure mode  | Overfitting / lack of theory       | Bias / invalid inference                  |

---

# **Mental shortcut**

* **Ad hoc → “built for this case” (fragile design)**
* **Post hoc → “explained after the fact” (fragile inference)**

---

# **Worst-case reviewer verdict**

> “The approach is ad hoc and the conclusions are largely post hoc.”

👉 Translation:

* Method is not principled
* Claims are not credible
